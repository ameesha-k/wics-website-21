What is WiCS Hacks?

Hosted by Texas Women in Computer Science

Women in Computer Science (WiCS) is a non-profit organization dedicated to building a network and community of women. Our mission is to encourage and support women in computing through: outreach, professional development, academic initiatives, and social events.

WiCS Hacks is our annual 2-day hackathon for all women and non-binary students held on-campus at The University of Texas at Austin. It's a place for students to learn new skills and come up with creative solutions using technology. To help along the way, we provide mentorship and workshops. Beginners are welcome!

  • Learn new skills and practice problem-solving.
  • Create products and services solving real-world problems.
  • Meet company sponsors and engineers in industry.

FAQ

If you don't see an answer you're looking for here, feel free to reach out!

  • When is WiCS Hacks?

    It'll be February 8-9, 2020! Exact schedule listed above!

  • Who can attend?

    All women and non-binary students are welcome to attend! If you are under 18 you must be a high school student admitted to the University of Texas at Austin.

  • Where will WiCS Hacks be held?

    WiCS Hacks will be hosted at the Gates Dell Complex at the University of Texas at Austin, TX. Please check-in in the atrium!

  • What should I bring?

    Yourself, a valid form of ID, laptop, chargers, and anything else you might need for the two days.

  • How much will this cost me?

    Nothing! It's completely free! WiCS provides students with Wi-Fi, meals, swag, workspace, and prizes for all of our events!

  • What projects can I work on?

    Whatever you'd like. Software, hardware, design, music, art, animation — take your pick. We will reveal the prize categories at the start of the hackathon.

  • How will teams work?

    Teams should be groups of up to 4 students. You can also work alone, but we encourage collaborating with the other great minds at WiCS Hacks.

  • What do I wear?

    Wear whatever you are most comfortable in! No need to wear anything professional.
